# Guardian Demo Director's Package

This package directs a 1 minute 35 second product demo recorded from the live Guardian deployment. It is designed for a calm founder voice, a clean browser capture, and restrained editing. It does not require any product changes.

## 1. Demo philosophy

The story is not that Guardian can generate strategy quickly. The story is that a strategic conclusion should be earned: Guardian listens, keeps uncertainty visible, compares explanations, and publishes a Decision Brief only when the evidence supports it.

The final feeling should be quiet relief. The founder is still responsible for the decision, but no longer has to hold every strategic uncertainty alone.

**The line to remember:** _Good decisions are earned, not generated._

## 2. Story structure

| Act        | Purpose                                                                                                  | Emotional movement                    |
| ---------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Opening    | Name the founder's real problem: scattered context and premature AI answers.                             | Curiosity → Recognition               |
| Learning   | Show that Guardian asks purposeful questions and explains why they matter.                               | Recognition → Confidence              |
| Readiness  | Make the transition from information to judgment visibly earned.                                         | Confidence → Understanding            |
| Judgment   | Reveal the evidence-led strategic view, alternatives, confidence rationale, and next learning objective. | Understanding → Trust                 |
| Continuity | Close on the promise that Guardian preserves the evolution of thinking without replacing the founder.    | Trust → Respect → Relief → Aspiration |

## 3. Demo scenario

Use this fictional company consistently. It is deliberately specific enough to produce useful reasoning while remaining an early-stage company with honest unknowns.

| Guardian question     | Founder response to paste                                                                                                                                                                               |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Problem               | Freelancers spend hours each month categorising expenses, reconciling transactions, and chasing tax-ready records. The work is repetitive, error-prone, and takes attention away from paid client work. |
| Customer              | Independent freelancers and one-person service businesses who manage their own books and have irregular income. The cost is highest near month-end and tax deadlines.                                   |
| Business Model        | A self-serve subscription product priced per freelancer, starting with a free trial. Accountants may eventually refer clients, but the initial path is direct.                                          |
| Current Stage         | We have a working prototype and ten interview participants. We are testing whether people will connect their accounts and return each month; we do not yet have paid retention.                         |
| Competitive Advantage | The initial advantage is a workflow that turns transaction context into explainable, tax-ready decisions for freelancers. This is still a hypothesis: trust and recurring use must be earned.           |

Do not add invented traction, revenue, or certainty to make the output sound stronger. The scenario is meant to demonstrate disciplined judgment, not a success story.

## 5. Recording script

### Recording setup

- Use a desktop browser at **1920 × 1080**, zoom set to 100%, and a fresh private/incognito window.
- Open only the Guardian tab. Hide bookmarks, extensions, notifications, and the operating-system taskbar.
- Use the live deployment: `https://guardian-pi-navy.vercel.app/`.
- Keep the mouse movement deliberate. Move the cursor into position, pause for 250 ms, click once, then leave it still while content changes.
- Record one uninterrupted master take. The edit should remove typing time and provider waiting; it should not fake any state or alter the product's output.

### Exact interaction order

1. Load the page and wait 2 seconds. Do not move the cursor.
2. Move to **Begin with what matters**, pause, then click once.
3. On the Problem question, pause for 1 second so the question and “Why this matters” can be read. Click the writing surface and paste the Problem response. Pause 1 second. Click **Share with Guardian**.
4. On the reflection, wait 1.5 seconds. Click **Continue learning**.
5. Repeat the following for Customer, Business Model, Current Stage, and Competitive Advantage:
   - pause for 0.8 seconds on the question;
   - paste the matching response;
   - pause for 0.8 seconds;
   - click **Share with Guardian**;
   - wait 1.2 seconds on the reflection;
   - click **Continue learning**. On the final reflection, click **Review the working picture**.
6. On **Current working picture**, pause for 3 seconds. Do not open edits. Click **Yes, that's accurate**.
7. On the readiness transition, pause for 2 seconds over the Learning / Readiness / Decision Brief explanation. Click **Begin evidence review**.
8. Let the live reasoning states run. Capture at least one second of each visible state. When the Intelligence view arrives, do not click until the current view has settled.
9. Capture the Current Strategic View and confidence rationale for 4 seconds. Make one slow vertical scroll so the Decision Brief heading, focus, and rationale are visible.
10. Open **Alternative interpretation**, pause 2 seconds, then open **What could change this focus**, pause 2 seconds.
11. Scroll to **Decision context**. Pause 3 seconds on the next learning objective or question.
12. Optionally capture the **Strategic Journal** control for a half-second cutaway. Do not make the main narrative depend on a historical transition: a new private session may correctly have no evolution yet.

### Recording contingency

If live reasoning stays in Learning Mode, returns an unavailable state, or does not publish a Decision Brief:

1. Stop the take; do not record an improvised workaround.
2. Check the deployed provider configuration and retry later in a new private session.
3. If the provider still withholds judgment, use the Learning Mode state only if the narration explicitly says Guardian is withholding judgment because the evidence is not sufficient. Never present that as a successful Decision Brief demo.

## 6. Narration script

Read at roughly 125–135 words per minute. Use an even, quiet pace. Leave the marked pauses; silence is part of the demo.

| Time        | Narration                                                                                                                                                     |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 00:00–00:07 | Founders rarely lack information. The harder problem is knowing what deserves confidence before a decision is made.                                           |
| 00:07–00:19 | Guardian begins by learning the company. Each question explains the uncertainty it is trying to reduce, so the conversation never becomes a questionnaire.    |
| 00:19–00:40 | As context arrives, Guardian builds a working picture. The founder can refine it at any point; nothing is treated as final simply because it was said once.   |
| 00:40–00:49 | Before reasoning begins, Guardian reflects that picture back. The founder remains in control of what the model understands.                                   |
| 00:49–00:58 | Only then does Guardian review the evidence, compare competing explanations, and check whether a strategic judgment has been earned.                          |
| 00:58–01:05 | It does not rush from information to advice. It first separates evidence, assumptions, uncertainty, and alternatives.                                         |
| 01:05–01:18 | When the evidence is sufficient, Guardian publishes a provisional Decision Brief. The strategic focus is clear, but its confidence is explained—not asserted. |
| 01:18–01:27 | Alternative interpretations stay visible, along with the evidence that could change the current view.                                                         |
| 01:27–01:35 | Guardian does not replace founder judgment. It gives founders a clearer basis for it. Good decisions are earned, not generated.                               |

## 7. Editing timeline

| Segment              | Edit direction                                                                                                                                    |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Opening              | Start on a clean screen. One 250 ms fade from black; no logo animation.                                                                           |
| Typing               | Use hard cuts or a restrained 6–8× speed ramp only across the paste/typing itself. Never speed up Guardian's reflection or reasoning states.      |
| Learning montage     | Use straight cuts between the four later questions. Keep each shot long enough to read the question and see that a response was supplied.         |
| Readiness transition | Use a single 150 ms dissolve after clicking Begin evidence review.                                                                                |
| Provider wait        | Retain at least one beat of each named reasoning state; remove dead waiting time between them.                                                    |
| Intelligence view    | Do not use kinetic typography or animated overlays. A single 104% crop is permitted for legibility; otherwise preserve the live UI at full frame. |
| End card             | Plain Porcelain background, Guardian Blue dot, “Guardian” in Inter, then the final line. Hold 2 seconds before fading out.                        |

Do not add fake cursor highlights, whooshes, neon effects, fake terminal footage, or feature labels that the product does not display.

## 8. Music and audio direction

- **Music:** sparse ambient piano or soft, warm electronic texture; 70–85 BPM; no vocals; no dramatic rise or drop.
- **Mix:** music begins 8–10 dB below narration and stays there. Lower it by a further 2 dB during the Decision Brief reveal.
- **Silence:** retain 0.5–1 second of natural silence after the opening line, before the readiness transition, and before the final line.
- **Interface sound:** none by default. If a subtle click is used, use one quiet, neutral click only; never add synthetic “AI” sounds.
- **Voice:** close, dry, and natural. Avoid heavy compression, radio effects, or theatrical delivery.

Use only licensed or royalty-free music and retain the license information with the final project files.

## 9. Thumbnail concept

**Composition:** a calm crop of the Current Strategic View on the left, with the Guardian Blue dot and wordmark in the upper-right. Keep the background Porcelain. Add only one line of text in Inter Semibold:

> Decisions are earned.

No person cutouts, arrows, exaggerated reaction faces, gradients, badges, or more than six words of text. The thumbnail should look like a product document, not a video advertisement.

## 10. Production checklist

### Before recording

- [ ] Confirm the live URL loads in a fresh private/incognito browser window.
- [ ] Confirm the deployed reasoning provider has a valid key, model access, and available quota.
- [ ] Complete one dry run using the exact fictional scenario in this document.
- [ ] Close unrelated tabs and applications; disable notifications and browser extensions.
- [ ] Set browser zoom to 100% and screen resolution to 1920 × 1080.
- [ ] Select a quiet room and test the microphone at the final speaking distance.
- [ ] Prepare the five response blocks for paste, in the order shown above.
- [ ] Record a 10-second test clip and check that text is legible at 1080p.

### During recording

- [ ] Start with a fresh session so the story begins at Guardian Learns.
- [ ] Follow the exact interaction order; do not improvise product claims.
- [ ] Capture the real loading states and the real reasoning output.
- [ ] Keep the cursor slow and purposeful.
- [ ] Record two clean master takes before editing.

### Export

- [ ] Export MP4, H.264, 1920 × 1080, 30 fps, progressive scan.
- [ ] Use a high-quality bitrate appropriate to the editor; avoid visible text compression.
- [ ] Export captions as a separate `.srt` file and, if desired, burn in restrained captions after reviewing their placement.
- [ ] Name the final file clearly: `guardian-build-week-demo.mp4`.

## 11. Final QA checklist

- [ ] The opening makes it clear that Guardian learns before it concludes.
- [ ] The demo shows why questions are asked, not merely that they are asked.
- [ ] The founder's ability to confirm and revise the working picture is visible.
- [ ] Learning → Readiness → Decision Brief is clear and unhurried.
- [ ] The Decision Brief shows confidence with a rationale, alternative interpretation, and conditions for change.
- [ ] The narration never claims Guardian is certain, autonomous, or a replacement for the founder.
- [ ] The video contains no API keys, personal data, notification banners, browser chrome, or unrelated tabs.
- [ ] Audio is clean, captions match narration, and music never competes with the voice.
- [ ] Text remains legible on a laptop at normal viewing size.
- [ ] The final line is exactly: “Good decisions are earned, not generated.”
- [ ] The final MP4 plays correctly from start to finish after upload or download.
