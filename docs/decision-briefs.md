# Decision Briefs

## Summary

This document explains how Guardian earns the right to publish a strategic judgment. A Decision Brief is not advice; it is a structured, provisional decision context.

## Learning Mode and Decision Mode

Guardian begins in **Learning Mode**. It moves to **Decision Mode** only when a deterministic readiness gate finds enough evidence to publish responsibly.

Readiness evaluates:

- evidence sufficiency;
- evidence consistency;
- hypothesis separation;
- critical unknowns; and
- decision stability.

Material ambiguity, insufficient evidence, or indistinguishable alternatives keep Guardian in Learning Mode.

## Decision Brief contents

When readiness is earned, a Decision Brief contains:

- current strategic focus;
- why it matters;
- supporting evidence;
- an alternative interpretation;
- remaining uncertainty;
- confidence and readiness;
- the next learning objective; and
- conditions that would change the focus.

Guardian may represent two inseparable concerns as a linked decision pair, but only when the available evidence cannot responsibly separate them.

## Publication gate

Publication is not a provider decision. Guardian derives readiness after validating `ReasoningOutput`, which keeps the gate consistent across providers and protects the product from persuasive but weakly grounded output.

## Lifecycle

Decision Briefs are versioned. A new version appears only after a meaningful strategic change, such as a different focus, material confidence movement, resolution of a linked pair, or resolution of a material contradiction. New information alone is not enough.

History is session-backed in the Build Week version. The domain model is deliberately ready for persistent storage in a future mission.
