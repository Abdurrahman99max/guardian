# Guardian Demo Conversation Script

This is the canonical founder conversation for Guardian's Build Week recording. It is the single source of truth for the Ledgerline scenario, pasted responses, expected progression, and recording recovery paths. Use it with the [Demo Director's Package](./demo-directors-package.md).

## Founder scenario

**Founder:** Maya Okafor, a former freelance product designer who has managed the finances of her own independent practice for seven years.

**Company:** Ledgerline, an AI bookkeeping assistant for freelancers and one-person service businesses.

**Current stage:** An evidence-backed early commercial product. Ledgerline has an eighteen-month paid cohort, measured conversion and retention, and defined unit economics for one focused customer segment.

**Strategic problem:** Maya needs a disciplined view of whether Ledgerline's observed subscription economics and retained customer value justify treating its current high-volume freelancer segment as the strategic focus.

**Why Maya uses Guardian:** She does not need a growth prescription. She needs a provisional, evidence-led decision context that explains what the observed economics support, what remains uncertain, and what evidence could change that view.

## Recording contract

- Paste every response below verbatim. Do not add extra metrics, investor language, or a claim of certainty.
- The figures are fictional demo evidence. Guardian must still treat them as founder claims, not independently verified facts.
- The exact strategic-focus wording is provider-generated. Record the live output rather than trying to force a title.
- The expected successful path ends in a provisional Decision Brief, never a recommendation or action plan.
- The first session creates Decision Brief Version 1 in the session-backed Strategic Journal. It will not have prior evolution to show.

## Complete conversation

### 1. Problem

**Guardian asks:** What problem is important enough for your company to solve?

**Founder response:**

> Freelancers with recurring client work and at least 30 monthly transactions spend hours categorising expenses, reconciling transactions, and preparing tax-ready records. The work is repetitive, error-prone, and takes attention away from paid client work.

**Why it exists:** Defines a costly recurring workflow for a precise segment without treating it as independently validated.

**UI progression:** Problem enters the Working Picture. Guardian reflects its current understanding, then the founder clicks **Continue learning**.

**Recording checkpoint:** B — after the reflection.

### 2. Customer

**Guardian asks:** Who experiences this problem most directly?

**Founder response:**

> Ledgerline serves independent designers, consultants, and marketers with recurring client income and at least 30 monthly transactions. A 2025 Freelancers Union survey of 2,400 independent professionals found 1,080 respondents in this transaction profile, and our current customers match that profile.

**Why it exists:** Resolves the earlier broad-market ambiguity by defining the same segment used in the commercial evidence.

**UI progression:** Customer joins Problem in the Working Picture. Click **Continue learning** after Guardian's reflection.

**Recording checkpoint:** C — after the reflection.

### 3. Business Model

**Guardian asks:** How do you expect the company to create and capture value?

**Founder response:**

> Ledgerline is a self-serve subscription priced at $24 per freelancer each month after a 14-day trial. Across the full paid subscriber base over the last eighteen months, blended acquisition cost is $18, gross margin is 82 percent, monthly logo churn is 2.3 percent, and observed customer lifetime value is $286. Customers are acquired directly through two independent freelancer communities; accountant referrals are not part of the current acquisition case.

**Why it exists:** Supplies the measured economics that were previously missing while explicitly keeping an untested acquisition channel out of the current case.

**UI progression:** Business Model joins the Working Picture. Click **Continue learning** after reflection.

**Recording checkpoint:** D — after the reflection.

### 4. Current Stage

**Guardian asks:** What is most true about the company's current stage?

**Founder response:**

> Over the last eighteen months, 520 freelancers matching the 30-transaction profile started a trial and 317 converted to paid. Two hundred and thirty-nine of those paid subscribers remain active after twelve months. Across the whole paid base, monthly logo churn has remained between 2.1 and 2.5 percent for the last six consecutive months. Retained subscribers report a median 2.3-hour reduction in month-end bookkeeping time. We do not offer the product to low-volume freelancers because the product is intentionally designed for the defined high-volume segment.

**Why it exists:** Resolves the material publication blockers: willingness to pay, retention horizon, whole-base churn, and the relationship between the product and its defined customer.

**UI progression:** Current Stage joins the Working Picture. This is evidence sufficient for a Moderate-confidence conclusion, not certainty. Click **Continue learning** after reflection.

**Recording checkpoint:** E — after the reflection.

### 5. Competitive Advantage

**Guardian asks:** What could make your company difficult to replace if it works?

**Founder response:**

> Ledgerline turns transaction context into explainable, tax-ready decisions. In structured interviews with forty retained subscribers, thirty-four cited the explanations and month-end time saving as the reason they continued rather than returning to spreadsheets. This supports a differentiated workflow for the defined customer; it does not claim an unassailable moat.

**Why it exists:** Connects retained customer behaviour to the workflow while preserving appropriate uncertainty about defensibility.

**UI progression:** All five areas have context. Click **Review the working picture** after Guardian's reflection.

**Recording checkpoint:** F — after the final reflection.

## Working Picture, readiness, and publication

### 6. Working Picture

Pause on **Current working picture**, let the five areas be read, and click **Yes, that's accurate**.

**Why it exists:** The founder confirms the working model before Guardian reasons from it. This preserves founder agency.

**Recording checkpoint:** G — before confirmation.

### 7. Readiness

On **An initial understanding is in place**, pause over **Learning -> Readiness -> Decision Brief**, then click **Begin evidence review**.

**Why it exists:** It visibly separates collecting context from publishing a strategic judgment.

**Recording checkpoint:** H — before the click.

### 8. Live reasoning

Capture **Reviewing evidence**, **Comparing explanations**, and **Preparing decision context**. Do not interact while these states are visible.

**Why it exists:** Makes Guardian's discipline legible without turning the experience into a chat transcript.

**Recording checkpoint:** I — once the Intelligence view arrives.

### 9. Decision Brief and Strategic Journal

The expected live shape is a Moderate-confidence, provisional view linking Ledgerline's subscription economics and the retained value of its workflow for the defined customer. The exact wording may vary.

Hold **Current strategic view** and its confidence rationale. Scroll to **Decision Brief · Version 1**, then open **Alternative interpretation** and **What could change this focus**. Finish on **Decision context** and capture the **Strategic Journal** control.

**Expected progression:** Decision Mode -> Decision Brief Version 1 -> current Strategic Journal entry. Recent evolution is empty in a first session because no earlier brief has been superseded.

**Recording checkpoint:** J — after the Decision Brief and Journal entry are visible.

## Expected reasoning discipline

- Every metric remains a founder claim, not independent confirmation.
- The customer definition, acquisition path, subscription economics, retention data, and workflow evidence all refer to the same high-volume freelancer segment.
- Guardian should publish only a provisional Moderate-confidence view.
- Guardian must leave external verification, competitive risk, and conditions for change visible.
- Guardian must stop at a Decision Brief and next learning objective; it must not produce a roadmap, pricing prescription, or growth plan.

## Recovery paths

### Guardian remains in Learning Mode

1. Confirm every response was pasted verbatim in a fresh private session.
2. Record the Learning Mode explanation only if the final film is explicitly about Guardian withholding judgment.
3. Do not rewrite the scenario or lower Guardian's publication standards to obtain a Decision Brief.

### The strategic focus differs

Keep the take if the output is grounded in supplied evidence, preserves alternatives and uncertainty, and remains non-advisory. Reject it only if it invents facts, claims certainty, issues recommendations, or contradicts the scenario.

### Provider is unavailable

Stop the take. Do not cut an error state into the demo. Verify the live provider, wait for recovery, and begin again in a fresh session.
