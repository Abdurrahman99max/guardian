# Guardian Demo Conversation Script

This is the canonical conversation for recording Guardian's Build Week demo. It is the single source of truth for founder inputs, on-screen progression, and recovery paths. The [Demo Director's Package](./demo-directors-package.md) covers camera, narration, editing, and export; it should be followed alongside this script.

## 1. Founder scenario

**Founder:** Maya Okafor, a former freelance product designer who has managed the finances of her own independent practice for seven years.

**Company:** Ledgerline, an AI bookkeeping assistant for freelancers and one-person service businesses.

**Current stage:** An early pilot. Twelve freelancers are actively testing the product; early usage is promising, but paid retention has not been established.

**Strategic problem:** Maya can see that manual bookkeeping is painful, but she does not yet know whether the limiting constraint is trust, recurring product value, pricing, or the path to acquiring freelancers.

**Why Maya uses Guardian:** She does not need a quick answer about growth. She needs a disciplined first view of which uncertainty matters most before she invests more time in product, distribution, or pricing.

## 2. Recording contract

- Use every response below verbatim. Paste responses rather than typing them live.
- Do not add unsupported metrics, investor language, or a claim of product-market fit.
- Guardian's intelligence output is live and may vary in wording. Record its real output; do not try to force a particular strategic-focus title.
- The canonical successful path ends with a **provisional Decision Brief**, not a recommendation or action plan.
- This first session may create **Decision Brief Version 1** but will not necessarily show a Strategic Journal transition. That is correct: evolution requires a meaningful later change.

## 4. Expected reasoning discipline

The demo is successful only if the live output preserves these boundaries:

- Pilot usage is treated as a **founder claim**, not independent confirmation.
- The stated two-hour time saving is useful evidence, not proof of a scalable retention engine.
- Accountant referrals remain a future assumption, not a validated acquisition channel.
- The workflow advantage remains a hypothesis until trust and recurring use are demonstrated.
- A Moderate confidence conclusion is credible. High confidence would be a warning sign in this scenario unless independently confirmed evidence has been introduced.
- Guardian should stop at a Decision Brief and next learning objective. It must not produce a product roadmap, pricing prescription, or growth plan.

## 5. Alternate recovery paths

### A. Guardian remains in Learning Mode

This is acceptable if the provider identifies a material ambiguity or does not sufficiently separate alternatives.

1. Keep the recorded Learning Mode explanation only if the final video is deliberately framed around Guardian withholding judgment.
2. For the canonical Decision Brief demo, begin a new private session and repeat the exact scenario. Do not edit text to make claims stronger.
3. If the result repeats, verify the provider configuration and use a known successful clean take after the operational path is restored.

### B. Guardian's strategic focus differs from the expected shape

Keep it if it is grounded in the provided evidence and preserves alternatives, uncertainty, and a non-advisory Decision Brief. The demo is about method, not a predetermined conclusion.

Reject the take only if the output invents facts, claims certainty, produces recommendations, or contradicts the supplied scenario.

### C. Guardian identifies a material contradiction

Review the pasted responses for transcription errors. The canonical scenario is internally consistent: early pilot usage and no paid retention can coexist. If the same contradiction persists, use a fresh session and record the honest clarification state rather than hiding it.

### D. Provider is unavailable or delayed

Stop the take. Do not cut an error state into the main demo. Verify the deployed provider environment, wait for service recovery, and start a new session. The demo should show real live reasoning, not a staged substitute.
