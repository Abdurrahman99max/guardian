# Future direction

## Summary

This document describes Guardian's direction after Build Week without presenting future work as already delivered. It matters because the product vision is larger than any single demo while its current boundaries remain intentional.

## The direction

Guardian is becoming a continuously evolving strategic model of a company. Its long-term value comes from connecting validated context, strategic decisions, external change, and the reasons a view evolved over time.

The goal is not more AI activity. It is better founder judgment under uncertainty.

## Planned capability areas

- durable strategic continuity and longitudinal confidence;
- founder-controlled company memory;
- document understanding and optional contextual evidence;
- external context and relevant market monitoring;
- strategic guidance built on earned Decision Readiness;
- collaborative strategic understanding for teams.

## Deliberate boundaries today

The Build Week application does not yet provide durable storage, authentication, document ingestion, external monitoring, collaboration, autonomous execution, or recommendations. These are future capabilities, not hidden promises.

## Design test

Any future capability should strengthen at least one of these outcomes:

1. Guardian understands the company more faithfully.
2. The founder understands the business more clearly.
3. The long-term strategic relationship becomes more trustworthy.

If it does none of those things, it does not belong in Guardian.
