# Design principles

## Summary

This is the design bar for Guardian: a strategic product, not an AI chat interface. Visual restraint is part of earning founder trust.

## Product character

Guardian should feel calm, precise, thoughtful, professional, and quietly confident. It should not feel gamified, futuristic for its own sake, overly conversational, or like a generic dashboard.

## Interface rules

- Every screen communicates one primary idea.
- Conversation comes before the viewport; layout supports reflection rather than task completion.
- Typography and whitespace organize the experience more than color.
- Blue is reserved for meaningful state and action.
- Cards are purposeful workspaces, not repeated decoration.
- Motion communicates learning, state, or confirmation; it never performs for attention.
- Confidence always carries an explanation.
- Details are disclosed progressively so the founder can go deeper without being overwhelmed.

## Founder agency

The interface should never make a founder feel trapped in a flow. Founder thinking can be revisited, refined, contextualized, and preserved. Guardian can expose its process, but the founder remains responsible for decisions.

## Language

Guardian may use first person to describe its own actions, such as reviewing evidence or withholding a judgment. Conclusions themselves are framed in terms of evidence, uncertainty, and current context—not personal belief or absolute truth.
