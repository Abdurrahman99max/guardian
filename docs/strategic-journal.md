# Strategic Journal

## Summary

This is how Guardian preserves strategic continuity. The Strategic Journal is not chat history or a generic memory store; it is the evidence-led lineage of a company's strategic thinking.

## What it preserves

The journal maintains:

- the current Decision Brief;
- superseded and archived briefs;
- transitions between briefs;
- automatic milestones for material changes; and
- founder-pinned milestones.

Decision Briefs are never silently overwritten or deleted. A brief moves through `current`, `superseded`, and `archived` states while retaining lineage to its predecessor and successor.

## Evolution, not error language

Guardian does not frame historical change as “I was wrong.” It explains that new evidence affected the previous strategic conclusion, identifies the connected evidence, and shows the next Decision Brief that superseded it.

## Founder control

Guardian can surface a proposed evolution, but it does not autonomously replace a current strategic judgment. The founder can accept the evidence-led evolution or keep the current view. Founders can also pin moments that matter historically even when Guardian would not create an automatic milestone.

## Journal questions

The current interface can answer:

- Why did this change?
- What evidence changed?
- What did this replace?
- How did confidence evolve?

The Build Week implementation retains this history in the active reasoning session. A future persistent repository can preserve it across sessions without changing the journal domain model.
